# Phase 2: Harmonic Depth (Weeks 5-8)
## Latin Jazz Improvisation - Practice Sheets

**Goal:** Navigate ii-V-i progressions in all keys with latin jazz scale choices
**Daily time:** 30-45 minutes
**Key concept:** Each chord in ii-V-i gets its own scale color

---

## NEW Scale 1: SPANISH / JEWISH (Phrygian Dominant)
**Use over:** V7b9 chords resolving to minor - THE latin dominant sound
**Formula:** H m3 H W H W W
**PDF Reference:** Section 2 - Spanish / Jewish

This is the 5th mode of Harmonic Minor. It has that unmistakable Afro-Cuban/tango flavor.

| Key | Notes | Resolves to |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| C7b9 | C Db E F G Ab Bb | F minor |
| Db7b9 | Db Ebb F Gb Ab Bbb Cb | Gb minor |
| D7b9 | D Eb F# G A Bb C | G minor |
| Eb7b9 | Eb Fb G Ab Bb Cb Db | Ab minor |
| E7b9 | E F G# A B C D | A minor |
| F7b9 | F Gb A Bb C Db Eb | Bb minor |
| F#7b9 | F# G A# B C# D E | B minor |
| G7b9 | G Ab B C D Eb F | C minor |
| Ab7b9 | Ab Bbb C Db Eb Fb Gb | Db minor |
| A7b9 | A Bb C# D E F G | D minor |
| Bb7b9 | Bb Cb D Eb F Gb Ab | Eb minor |
| B7b9 | B C D# E F# G A | E minor |

### The Sound: That b9 interval (root to Db over C7) is what makes salsa, mambo, and tango harmony so distinctive.

## NEW Scale 4: BEBOP DOMINANT
**Use over:** dominant 7th chords - adds chromatic passing tone for smooth eighth notes
**Formula:** W W H W W H H H (8-note scale)
**PDF Reference:** Section 2 - Bebop (Dominant)

| Key | Notes (8 notes) |
|-----|----------------|
| C7 | C D E F G A Bb B |
| Db7 | Db Eb F Gb Ab Bb Cb C |
| D7 | D E F# G A B C C# |
| Eb7 | Eb F G Ab Bb C Db D |
| E7 | E F# G# A B C# D D# |
| F7 | F G A Bb C D Eb E |
| F#7 | F# G# A# B C# D# E E# |
| G7 | G A B C D E F F# |
| Ab7 | Ab Bb C Db Eb F Gb G |
| A7 | A B C# D E F# G G# |
| Bb7 | Bb C D Eb F G Ab A |
| B7 | B C# D# E F# G# A A# |

### Key insight: The added natural 7th means chord tones fall on downbeats when playing continuous eighth notes. This is what makes bebop lines sound "right."

## MASTER EXERCISE: ii-V-i in ALL 12 MINOR KEYS

This is the core latin jazz progression. Practice each row as a unit.

| Key Center | ii chord (Dorian) | V chord (Spanish/Jewish) | i chord (Harmonic Minor) |
|------------|------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| **C minor** | D-7 Dorian: D E F G A B C | G7b9 Spanish: G Ab B C D Eb F | C harm min: C D Eb F G Ab B |
| **Db minor** | Eb-7 Dorian: Eb F Gb Ab Bb C Db | Ab7b9 Spanish: Ab Bbb C Db Eb Fb Gb | Db harm min: Db Eb Fb Gb Ab Bbb C |
| **D minor** | E-7 Dorian: E F# G A B C# D | A7b9 Spanish: A Bb C# D E F G | D harm min: D E F G A Bb C# |
| **Eb minor** | F-7 Dorian: F G Ab Bb C D Eb | Bb7b9 Spanish: Bb Cb D Eb F Gb Ab | Eb harm min: Eb F Gb Ab Bb Cb D |
| **E minor** | F#-7 Dorian: F# G# A B C# D# E | B7b9 Spanish: B C D# E F# G A | E harm min: E F# G A B C D# |
| **F minor** | G-7 Dorian: G A Bb C D E F | C7b9 Spanish: C Db E F G Ab Bb | F harm min: F G Ab Bb C Db E |
| **F# minor** | G#-7 Dorian: G# A# B C# D# E# F# | D#7b9 Spanish: D# E F## G# A# B C# | F# harm min: F# G# A B C# D E# |
| **G minor** | A-7 Dorian: A B C D E F# G | D7b9 Spanish: D Eb F# G A Bb C | G harm min: G A Bb C D Eb F# |
| **Ab minor** | Bb-7 Dorian: Bb C Db Eb F G Ab | Eb7b9 Spanish: Eb Fb G Ab Bb Cb Db | Ab harm min: Ab Bb Cb Db Eb Fb G |
| **A minor** | B-7 Dorian: B C# D E F# G# A | E7b9 Spanish: E F G# A B C D | A harm min: A B C D E F G# |
| **Bb minor** | C-7 Dorian: C D Eb F G A Bb | F7b9 Spanish: F Gb A Bb C Db Eb | Bb harm min: Bb C Db Eb F Gb A |
| **B minor** | C#-7 Dorian: C# D# E F# G# A# B | F#7b9 Spanish: F# G A# B C# D E | B harm min: B C# D E F# G A# |

### Practice Method:
1. Play each scale ascending/descending over the chord
2. Then connect them: 4 beats Dorian, 4 beats Spanish, 8 beats Harmonic Minor
3. Use the Bebop Dominant scale as an alternate on the V chord for smoother lines
4. Over static dominant vamps, try Lydian Dominant instead

## Week-by-Week Schedule

### Week 5: Learn Spanish/Jewish in 4 keys (G, D, A, C)
- Play V7b9 resolving to i minor
- Compare sound to plain Mixolydian - hear the difference!

### Week 6: Add Harmonic Minor + connect ii-V-i
- Practice the full ii-V-i in C minor, G minor, D minor, F minor
- Dorian (4 beats) -> Spanish (4 beats) -> Harmonic Minor (8 beats)

### Week 7: Add Melodic Minor + Bebop Dominant
- Use Melodic Minor on the i chord as an alternative to Harmonic Minor
- Use Bebop Dominant on the V chord for flowing eighth-note lines

### Week 8: All 12 keys + Lydian Dominant
- Cycle ii-V-i through all 12 minor keys
- Add Lydian Dominant over static dominant vamps
- Record yourself improvising over a latin ii-V-i backing track
