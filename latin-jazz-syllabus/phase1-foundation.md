# Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
## Latin Jazz Improvisation - Practice Sheets

**Goal:** Build core scale fluency in the most common Latin jazz keys
**Daily time:** 30-45 minutes
**Always practice with a clave pattern** (son clave 3-2 or 2-3)

---

### Priority Practice Order (most common latin keys first):
1. D Dorian (concert F/C minor)
2. G Dorian (concert Bb/F minor)
3. A Dorian (concert C/G minor)
4. E Dorian (concert G/D minor)
5. C Dorian (concert Eb/Bb minor)
6. F Dorian (concert Ab/Eb minor)
7. Bb Dorian (concert Db/Ab minor)
8. Then fill in remaining keys

### Practice Patterns:
1. Ascending/descending full range
2. In thirds: C-Eb, D-F, Eb-G, F-A, G-Bb, A-C, Bb-D
3. 1-2-3-1, 2-3-4-2, 3-4-5-3 sequences
4. Play with clave accents on beats that align with the clave

## Week-by-Week Schedule

### Week 1: D Dorian + G Mixolydian + D Minor Pentatonic
- These form a ii-V in C minor (concert key for tenor)
- Practice each scale 5 min with metronome + clave
- Then improvise over a Dm7-G7 vamp (10 min)

### Week 2: G Dorian + C Mixolydian + G Minor Pentatonic
- ii-V in F minor (concert key for tenor)
- Same routine as Week 1
- Add Blues scale over both chords

### Week 3: A Dorian + D Mixolydian + A Minor Pentatonic
- ii-V in G minor (concert key for tenor)
- Start connecting scales: Dorian on ii, Mixolydian on V

### Week 4: Review all 3 key centers + add E Dorian, C Dorian, F Dorian
- Cycle through all practiced keys
- Improvise freely over latin backing tracks
- Record yourself and listen back

## Clave Rhythm Reference

### Son Clave 3-2 (most common):
```
3-side: X . . X . . X .
2-side: . . X . . X . .
```

### Son Clave 2-3:
```
2-side: . . X . . X . .
3-side: X . . X . . X .
```

### Rumba Clave 3-2:
```
3-side: X . . X . . . X
2-side: . . X . . X . .
```

Practice tip: Tap the clave with your foot while playing scales.
The goal is to feel which notes "land" on clave beats and which are between.
