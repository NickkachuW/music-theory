## NEW Scale 1: DIMINISHED WHOLE TONE (Altered Scale)
**Use over:** V7#9, V7b9, V7#5 - maximum tension before resolving to i
**Formula:** H W H W W W W
**Also known as:** Altered scale, Super Locrian, 7th mode of Melodic Minor
**PDF Reference:** Section 2 - Diminished Whole Tone

This is the most "outside" sounding dominant scale. Every extension is altered.
It contains: root, b9, #9, 3, #11, b13, b7

| Key | Notes |
|-----|-------|
| C7alt | C Db D# E F# G# Bb |
| Db7alt | Db Ebb E F G A Cb |
| D7alt | D Eb E# F# G# A# C |
| Eb7alt | Eb Fb F# G A B Db |
| E7alt | E F F## G# A# B# D |
| F7alt | F Gb G# A B C# Eb |
| F#7alt | F# G A Bb C D E |
| G7alt | G Ab A# B C# D# F |
| Ab7alt | Ab Bbb B C D E Gb |
| A7alt | A Bb B# C# D# E# G |
| Bb7alt | Bb Cb C# D E F# Ab |
| B7alt | B C C## D# E# F## A |

### Latin Jazz Application:
Over a V7 chord, play the Altered scale for 2 beats, then resolve to the i chord.
The tension-release is what makes great latin jazz solos electrifying.

**Comparison exercise:** Play these 3 over the same V7 chord and hear the difference:
1. Mixolydian (vanilla)
2. Spanish/Jewish (spicy but stable)
3. Altered/Dim Whole Tone (maximum tension)

## NEW Scale 2: WHOLE TONE
**Use over:** V7#5 chords, augmented dominant chords, dreamy floating passages
**Formula:** W W W W W W (6 notes, all whole steps)
**PDF Reference:** Section 2 - Whole Tone

Only 2 whole tone scales exist (they just start on different notes):

**Whole Tone Scale 1:** C D E F# G# Bb (= D E F# G# Bb C = E F# G# Bb C D, etc.)
**Whole Tone Scale 2:** Db Eb F G A B (= Eb F G A B Db = F G A B Db Eb, etc.)

| Key | Notes | Which WT Scale |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| C7#5 | C D E F# G# Bb | WT 1 |
| Db7#5 | Db Eb F G A Cb | WT 2 |
| D7#5 | D E F# G# A# C | WT 1 |
| Eb7#5 | Eb F G A B Db | WT 2 |
| E7#5 | E F# G# A# B# D | WT 1 |
| F7#5 | F G A B C# Eb | WT 2 |
| F#7#5 | F# G# A# B# C## E | WT 1 |
| G7#5 | G A B C# D# F | WT 1 |
| Ab7#5 | Ab Bb C D E Gb | WT 2 |
| A7#5 | A B C# D# E# G | WT 1 |
| Bb7#5 | Bb C D E F# Ab | WT 1 |
| B7#5 | B C# D# E# F## A | WT 1 |

### Practice: Play the whole tone scale, then resolve to the tonic minor. The "floating" quality creates beautiful contrast.

## NEW Scale 3: DIMINISHED (begin with H) - Over Dominant b9
**Use over:** V7b9 chords - symmetric, mysterious, angular
**Formula:** H W H W H W H W (8-note scale)
**PDF Reference:** Section 2 - Diminished (begin with H)

This is a symmetric scale - only 3 unique diminished scales exist:

| Key | Notes (8 notes) |
|-----|----------------|
| C7b9 | C Db D# E F# G A Bb |
| Db7b9 | Db Ebb E F G Ab Bb Cb |
| D7b9 | D Eb E# F# G# A B C |
| Eb7b9 | Eb Fb F# G A Bb C Db |
| E7b9 | E F F## G# A# B C# D |
| F7b9 | F Gb G# A B C D Eb |
| F#7b9 | F# G A Bb C# D E F |
| G7b9 | G Ab A# B C# D E F |
| Ab7b9 | Ab Bbb B C D Eb F Gb |
| A7b9 | A Bb B# C# D# E F# G |
| Bb7b9 | Bb Cb C# D E F G Ab |
| B7b9 | B C C## D# E# F# G# A |

### Comparison with Spanish/Jewish:
Both work over V7b9, but:
- **Spanish/Jewish** = more melodic, vocal, traditional latin
- **Diminished (H-W)** = more angular, jazzy, modern

## TENSION-RESOLUTION EXERCISE (All 12 Keys)

Practice this pattern: 2 bars tension -> 2 bars resolution

| Key | Tension Scale (V7) | Resolution Scale (i) |
|-----|-------------------|---------------------|
| to C min | G Altered: G Ab A# B C# D# F | C Dorian: C D Eb F G A Bb |
| to Db min | Ab Altered: Ab Bbb B C D E Gb | Db Dorian: Db Eb Fb Gb Ab Bb Cb |
| to D min | A Altered: A Bb B# C# D# E# G | D Dorian: D E F G A B C |
| to Eb min | Bb Altered: Bb Cb C# D E F# Ab | Eb Dorian: Eb F Gb Ab Bb C Db |
| to E min | B Altered: B C C## D# E# F## A | E Dorian: E F# G A B C# D |
| to F min | C Altered: C Db D# E F# G# Bb | F Dorian: F G Ab Bb C D Eb |
| to F# min | C# Altered: C# D E F G A B | F# Dorian: F# G# A B C# D# E |
| to G min | D Altered: D Eb E# F# G# A# C | G Dorian: G A Bb C D E F |
| to Ab min | Eb Altered: Eb Fb F# G A B Db | Ab Dorian: Ab Bb Cb Db Eb F Gb |
| to A min | E Altered: E F F## G# A# B# D | A Dorian: A B C D E F# G |
| to Bb min | F Altered: F Gb G# A B C# Eb | Bb Dorian: Bb C Db Eb F G Ab |
| to B min | F# Altered: F# G A Bb C D E | B Dorian: B C# D E F# G# A |

## Week-by-Week Schedule

### Week 9: Altered Scale in 4 keys + tension-resolution exercise
- Learn G alt, D alt, A alt, C alt
- Practice: 2 bars Altered -> resolve to Dorian on the i chord

### Week 10: Whole Tone + Diminished (H-W) in 4 keys
- Compare all 3 dominant tensions: Altered vs Whole Tone vs Diminished
- Each has a different color - learn to choose by ear

### Week 11: Phrygian + Diminished (W-H) for minor chord color
- Practice Phrygian over dark modal vamps
- Use Diminished W-H as a brief "outside" gesture over minor chords

### Week 12: Integration
- Improvise over full latin tunes using ALL tension scales
- Record and compare: which tension scale sounds best in each context?
